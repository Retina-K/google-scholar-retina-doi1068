Abstract

This abstract is a reply to comments on the publication "Surgical outcome of simultaneous intraocular lens rescue and sutureless intrascleral tunnel fixation of dislocated intraocular lenses, M Kim, DH Lee, HJ Koh, SC Lee, SS Kim, Retina 35 (7), 1450-1457." The authors acknowledge limited IOL type information due to urgent patient management but assert long-term haptic stability if the tunnel is properly constructed. They emphasize the technique's advantage in avoiding suture-related complications, preventing hypotony, and offering a less invasive solution for three-piece IOLs that are not damaged, negating the need for large incisions or IOL exchange.
